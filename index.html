Vega

A bright, nearby star in the constellation Lyra, part of the Summer Triangle.

Type: A-Type Star

Location: 25 light-years

Notability: Bright anchor in the Summer Triangle

Size: Medium-sized main-sequence star

UY Scuti

One of the largest known stars by radius, a red supergiant in Scutum.

Type: Red Supergiant

Location: 9,500 light-years

Size: Gigantic beyond compare

Altair

A rapidly rotating star in the constellation Aquila.

Type: A-Type Star

Location: 16.7 light-years

Size: Slightly flattened due to spin

WASP-12b

A "hot Jupiter" so close to its star it is being pulled apart.

Type: Hot Jupiter

Location: 870 light-years

Size: Extremely large and inflated

Rigel

A massive blue-white supergiant in Orion.

Type: Blue Supergiant

Location: 860 light-years

Size: Massive and radiant

Betelgeuse

A red supergiant in Orion, nearing the end of its life.

Type: Red Supergiant

Location: 642 light-years

Size: Enormous stellar body

Kepler-452b

Nicknamed “Earth's cousin,” located in the habitable zone.

Type: Super-Earth Exoplanet

Location: 1,400 light-years

Size: Larger than Earth

TRAPPIST-1e

One of seven Earth-sized planets orbiting an ultra-cool dwarf star.

Type: Earth-like Exoplanet

Location: 40 light-years

Size: Similar to Earth

Gliese 581g

A controversial but often discussed candidate for habitability.

Type: Super-Earth Exoplanet

Location: 20.4 light-years

Size: Similar to or slightly larger than Earth
